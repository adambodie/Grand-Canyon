After returning back to our rooms at Mesquite, it was time to get ready to resume our day. With a few hours to spare before boarding the train, and limited transportation around the Grand Canyon, we needed a plan. But first, Mom had to make sure her Dad was okay, being in the hospital for the past few days for a staph infection in the elbow. She couldn't talk to her Dad, so instead she had to talk to her two bratty little brothers.

Of course, there are two things that will never ever change:

  1. Steve Sherman being obsessed with USC.
  2. Steve Sherman never saying anything nice to me and only making jokes about the Oregon Ducks.

This time though, I wasn't going to take it. Enough was enough. If he wants to be jealous because Oregon has a better football team than USC does now, that's fine, but he needs to accept the fact that his school is nothing more than mediocre football team whose only accomplishments were tainted by cheating. "It's better to have reached the title game and lose than to win the game and give it up for breaking the rules. USC should be more worried about even making the big game cuz they've in the middle of the pack for years. Don't mess with AB, and I'm out!"

Things that Annoy Me (The Grand Canyon Edition)

by Adam Bodie



Imagine being on a trip of a lifetime at a popular National Park. Naturally, there are going to be a lot of people visiting, just like you. Many of them wind up in your pictures. No matter how hard you try, someone enters your picture (just as someone else leaves). Often times, you walk into someone else's picture. You try your best to wait, or to walk fast to avoid ruining that other person's moment. But what happens when you're walking and someone is blocking your path? Do you wait? Do you walk fast? Enter the Selfie Stick...

Everyone loves to take selfies. It's easy, convenient and requires no stranger to get that special moment on camera for you. But it can only go the length of the longest arm you have. Enter the Selfie Stick, which can take selfies at a longer distance, to capture more stuff in the background. But what looks cool for the consumer is an eyesore for the passerby.

No longer can a person walk between the camera and the object of affection. Instead, there's a goofy looking stick covering that space. You'd have to walk around a longer distance to get around the picture. And what if someone decided the moment you walk by that they want to use it? They'd have to swing it to set it up, making sure they don't hit you in the face. Some people, though, arent that careful. Bam! Now you got hit in the face with a stick. A great way to end your trip, with marks on your face from Selfie Sticks.

Now I know what you're saying; Selfies are not going away, it's the 21st Century, yada yada yada. We guess what, there's a better solution then having some dorky looking stick take up so much space for the passerby. Have your loved one take a picture for you. Ask a stranger. Take a selfie without the stick. How much extra space do you really get in your photo? Chances are, you're not a good photographer to begin with. The picture is a little blurry because you can't stay still. Your finger winds up in the picture. You cut your head off like Hannibal Lecter. So please, leave the Selfie Stick at home and be thankful you can even have the technology to take a picture


Yavapai

The Yavapai is a Native American tribe in the region. Their name translates to "People of the Sun."

There are four bands of Yavapai, the Western Yavapai, the Northwestern Yavapai, the Southeastern Yavapai and Northeastern Yavapai.

There are multiple reservations for the Yavapai Tribe, including the Yavapai-Apache Nation Indian Reservation, the Yavapai Prescott Indian Reservation and Fort McDowell Reservation, all in Arizona.

The Yavapai were often mistaken for the neighboring Apache tribe.
