So here's what happened on the Train

Here are some of the highlights (and lowlights) riding the Train to the Grand Canyon

One thing that became a minor concern on the train was when Mike, the Train Guy, informed us that the toilets burp; therefore, we needed to keep the lid on when flushing. It also appeared that the bathroom nearest us was a dinky toilet, with a foldable sink. After passing the passerby's who love being waved at (making their day) and the Prairie Dog Village, where the Prairie Dog's roam, we were off to the Grand Canyon, with desert scenery and Ponderosa Pines that would make Bend, Oregon jealous with envy.

Some interesting facts at the Grand Canyon Railway

The whole route runs 64 miles, going from Williams, to the Grand Canyon

It began in 1901, built by the Santa Fe Railway, who owned it until 1968, when trains became less popular than automobiles.

The railway was eventually started up again after being purchased by Max and Thelma Biegert, a wealthy couple from Phoenix.

They eventually sold it to its current owners, the Xanterra Parks & Resorts group, originally known as the Fred Harvey Company.

Fred Harvey must be special, because that's the part of the train we were on!
